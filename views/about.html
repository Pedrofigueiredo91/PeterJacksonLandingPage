Here we talk his early, personal, and professional life

Early life

Jackson's Birth and Upbringing: Born on October 31, 1961, in Wellington, Peter Jackson grew up in the northern suburb of Pukerua Bay. His parents, Joan and William "Bill" Jackson, migrated from England, with Joan working as a housewife and factory worker, and Bill as a wages clerk. Passion for Cinema and Initial Filmmaking: From a young age, Jackson's fascination with cinema was evident. He immersed himself in Ray Harryhausen's films, Thunderbirds, and Monty Python's Flying Circus. A pivotal moment came when a family friend gifted them a Super 8 cine-camera, sparking Jackson's journey into crafting short films alongside friends. He even attempted to recreate his favorite film, King Kong, through stop-motion animation. Career

Peter Jackson's career is characterized by a diverse range of films that span various genres. He gained recognition with his early splatter phase, which included movies like "Bad Taste," "Meet the Feebles," and "Braindead." These films showcased his unique style and dark humor. Jackson's transition to more ambitious projects is evident in "Heavenly Creatures," a critically acclaimed drama based on a real murder case. His Hollywood success started with "The Frighteners," a comedy-horror film starring Michael J. Fox. Jackson's most iconic achievement is undoubtedly "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy, adapted from J.R.R. Tolkien's fantasy epic. The trilogy garnered widespread acclaim, winning multiple Academy Awards, including Best Picture for "The Return of the King." Jackson continued to showcase his directing prowess with the remake of "King Kong," which received positive reviews and achieved commercial success. He also produced and directed the documentary "They Shall Not Grow Old," a unique portrayal of World War I using restored archival footage. Jackson's interests extended to video games, co-creating Wingnut Interactive, and planning to work on a game set in the "Halo" universe. Additionally, he contributed to charitable activities and aviation heritage preservation. His cameos in films, attention to detail, and dedication to storytelling have solidified his legacy as a visionary filmmaker.

Personal life

Peter Jackson, along with his partner Dame Fran Walsh, is a prominent figure in the film industry. Walsh, a screenwriter, film producer, and lyricist, has been an essential collaborator on Jackson's projects since 1989. She contributed as a co-writer and producer, earning multiple Academy Awards for her work on "The Lord of the Rings" trilogy, including Best Picture, Best Adapted Screenplay, and Best Original Song. Jackson is known for his passion for aviation and owns a collection of over 40 World War I-era warbirds, which are housed at Hood Aerodrome near Masterton. He also possesses a Gulfstream G650 jet and the original Chitty Chitty Bang Bang car. He has a deep interest in building scale models and owns a company, Wingnut Wings, which specializes in crafting models of World War I aircraft. In addition, Jackson's involvement in preserving aviation history is evident through the Omaka Aviation Heritage Centre's Knights of the Sky exhibition, showcasing his own collection of WW1 aircraft and artifacts. This exhibition brings the story of aviation in World War I to life through sets created by his companies, WingNut Films and Weta Workshop. Jackson's influence extends beyond filmmaking, as his support for city councilor Andy Foster during the 2019 Wellington City Council Elections garnered attention. Foster's victory was aided by Jackson's endorsement and financial contribution to his campaign. Both Jackson and Foster had criticized a previous city council decision regarding property development at Shelly Bay.
